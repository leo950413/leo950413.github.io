Relation and function

Cartesian Product

Given to set $A,B$ the Cartesian Product of it is $$ A\times B = {(a,b)| a\in A , b\in B}$$

Ordered pair

If $ (a_1,b_1) = (a_2 , b_2) $ , then $a_1 = a_2 , \ b_1 =b_2 $.

Relation

$$ A:{1,2,3} $$ $$ B:{a,b,c} $$

$$A \times B = {(1,a) , (2,b) , (3,a) , (3,c)} $$

All subset of $A \times B $ is a relation from $ A $ to $ B $, ${(1,a) , (3,c)}$ is one of them. $ A \times A $ can written as $ A^2 $

Power set

Denote as $p(S)$ is the set of all possible subset of S The number of relation between $A$,$B$ $$ |A| = m , |B|= n $$ $$ P(A\times B) = 2 ^ {mn} $$

Function (mapping)

$$ A = {x_1 , x_2 , … , x_n}$$ $$ B = {y_1 , y_2 , … , y_n} $$ Every element of A appears exactly once as the first component of an ordered pair in the relation. A function from $A$ to $B$ is denoted as $f(A) = B$.

Range

The set of all values that the function actually outputs when applied to elements from the domain, It is a subset of the codomain.

Codomain

The set of all possible values that could theoretically be the output of the function.

For function $f(x) = x^2 , x\in\mathbb{Z}$

The codomain is $ {{x\in\mathbb{Z} , x>0}.} $

The range is ${x ^2 , x\in\mathbb{Z}}$

One to One (injective):

Every element in the domain maps to a unique element in the codomain.

$$A = {1, 2, 3} $$

$$B = {a,b,c,d} $$ $ f(A)\to B = {(1,a) , (2,b) , (3,c) }$ is a injective function.

$ g(A)\to B = {(1,a) , (2,b) , (3,b) }$ is not a injective function.

Note that $ f(A)\to B $ is not injective if $n(A) > n(B)$.

Onto (surjective)

Every element in the codomain has at least one corresponding element in the domain.

$$A = {1, 2, 3 , 4} $$

$$B = {a,b,c} $$ $ f(A)\to B = {(1,a) , (2,b) , (3,c) }$ is a injective function.

$ g(A)\to B = {(1,a) , (2,b) , (3,b) }$ is not a injective function. Note that $ f(A)\to B $ is not surjective if $n(B) > n(A)$.
