The derivative and integral of trig-function

Nov 15, 2024

The definition of arc-function

$$ \arcsin(\sin(x)) = x \quad \text{if } -\frac{\pi}{2} \leq x \leq \frac{\pi}{2} $$ $$ \arccos(\cos(x)) = x \quad \text{if } 0 \leq x \leq \pi $$ $$ \arctan(\tan(x)) = x \quad \text{if } -\frac{\pi}{2} < x < \frac{\pi}{2} $$

The derivative of inverse function

Let $ g(x) $ be the inverse function of $ f(x) $ $$ f(g(x)) = x $$ Differentiate both side using chain rule $$ f’(g(x))*g’(x) = 1 $$ $g’(x)$ is the derivative of $g(x)$ , so we can move everyting to the right side of equation. $$ g’(x) = \frac{1}{f’(g(x))} $$

The derivative of arcsin(x)

Let $ f(x) = sin(x) , g(x) = arcsin(x) $ $$ arcsin’(x) = \frac{1}{cos(arcsin(x))} $$ In the denominator, we have $cos(arcsin(x))$ also can be written like $$ \sqrt{1-sin^2(arcsin(x))} $$ Note that $$sin^2(arcsin(x) = (sin(arcsin(x))) ^ 2 $$ Which is equivalent to $x^2$ so we can derive the conclusion $$ arcsin(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} $$

The dervative of arctan(x)

Use the same formula we know that $$ arctan’(x) = \frac{1}{sec^2(arctan(x))}$$ From the trig identity $$ sec^2(x) = 1 + tan^2(x) $$ Let $m(x) = sec^2(x) $ , then subsitute $ sec^2(x) $ $$ arctan’(x) = \frac{1}{m(arctan(x))} $$ Plug $ arctan(x) $ to $m(x)$ $$ m(arctan(x)) = 1 + tan^2(arctan(x)) \=1 + x^2 $$ So the final formula is $$ arctan’(x) = \frac{1}{1 + x^2} $$

The u-subsitution

$$ \int f’(g(x))\cdot g’(x)\ dx = f(g(x)) $$

The integral of tan(x)

By the definition of $tan(x) = \frac{sin(x)}{cos(x)}$ $$\int tan(x)dx = \int \frac{sin(x)}{cos(x)} dx $$ Let $u = cos(x) , du = -sin(x)dx$ $$ \int -u^{-1}du = -ln(u) + c $$ Replace u with the original $cos(x) $ , then we have the integral of $tan(x)$ $$ \int tan(x)dx = -ln|cos(x)| +c $$

The integral of sec(x)

To find the integral of $ sec(x) $ , multiply $ \frac{sec(x) + tan(x)}{sec(x) + tan(x)} $

$$ \int sec(x)dx = \int \frac {sec^2(x) + sec(x)tan(x)}{sec(x)+tan(x)}dx $$ Let $u = sec(x) + tan(x) , du = (sec^2(x) + sec(x)tan(x))dx$ $$ \int sec(x)dx =\int \frac {1}{u} du = ln|u| + C =ln|sec(x)+tan(x) | + C $$
